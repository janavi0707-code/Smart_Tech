Differences between SDLC and Agile Methodologies

Structure

SDLC: Sequential and rigid process.

Agile: Iterative and flexible approach.

Flexibility

SDLC: Less adaptable to changes.

Agile: Highly adaptable and welcomes changes.

Customer Involvement

SDLC: Limited, mostly at the beginning and end.

Agile: Continuous feedback and involvement.

Timeframe

SDLC: Longer cycles with a single delivery.

Agile: Short sprints with frequent deliveries.

Risk

SDLC: Higher risk if requirements change late.

Agile: Lower risk due to early testing and feedback.

Best For

SDLC: Projects with clear, unchanging requirements.

Agile: Dynamic projects with evolving needs.
